Title: Protein Structure Prediction of Human Hemoglobin Beta Chain (HBB)

Objective:
To predict and analyze the three-dimensional structure of the Human Hemoglobin Beta Chain using AlphaFold DB and visualize the structure using molecular visualization software.

Protein Information:
Protein Name: Hemoglobin Subunit Beta (HBB)
Organism: Homo sapiens
UniProt ID: P68871

Methodology:
The amino acid sequence of HBB was obtained from UniProt. The predicted three-dimensional structure was downloaded from AlphaFold Protein Structure Database. The structure was visualized using PyMOL/Chimera software in cartoon representation.

Structural Features:

1. The protein is predominantly composed of alpha-helices.
2. Several helices are connected through loop regions.
3. The compact globular structure is characteristic of globin family proteins.
4. AlphaFold predicted the structure with high confidence for most residues.
5. The structure contributes to oxygen transport as part of hemoglobin in red blood cells.

Results:
The predicted HBB structure displayed the typical globin fold consisting mainly of alpha-helical segments. Visualization confirmed a compact and stable tertiary structure. Most residues showed high prediction confidence, indicating a reliable structural model.

Conclusion:
AlphaFold successfully predicted the three-dimensional structure of Human Hemoglobin Beta Chain. The protein exhibits the classical globin architecture essential for its biological role in oxygen transport.
